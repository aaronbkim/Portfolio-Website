Ethiopia Reads Commission

In Early October of 2020, I collaborated with Ethiopia Reads for a fundraiser. Over the course of a few days, we worked to create a t-shirt design that would encapsulate their values and inspirations.

Ethiopia Reads is a non-profit organization whose mission is to create a culture of reading in Ethiopia. The focus of the design was to be an Ethiopian angel surrounded by native flowers and the words የሚያነቡ ያብባሉ, which translate to “those who read, bloom". After going through an array of iterations over several back-and-forth emails, we agreed upon the final design. For a while, we weren't exactly sure how to orient the design on the shirt. One of the discussed options had it at the top corner, where it would be relatively small. And although I was a fan of how that looked, it seemed far more logical to place it across the middle of the shirt where it would be considerably larger and easier to read. I created some digital mock-ups displaying what it would look like on the shirts of real people, and when we were happy with everything, it was time to open up shop.

The design was scanned onto short and long-sleeved t-shirts, hoodies, and crewneck sweaters in five different colors, and made available for purchase. Despite the initial goal of 50, they were able to sell 170 shirts by the first week of November, raising over $2,000 (and counting).
